\subsection{INRIA dataset}

After achieving excellent results on the COIL100 dataset, we tried to get similar results on a more difficult dataset. The dataset we chose is the Head Pose Image Database \cite{estimating-face-orientation-inria} from the French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation (INRIA). The dataset consists of 15 persons, two series per person and 93 JPEG-images per series. In each series the depicted person changes gradually its posture of the head, in one of the series with a certain feature like glasses (e.g. person 1 and 4) or the hair outside of the bounds of the image (person 6) and in one series without. Figure \ref{fig:inria_different_angles} shoes some example images for person 12.

\begin{figure}[h!]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{inria_different_angles}
	\caption{Person 12 presented from different angles}
	\label{fig:inria_different_angles}
\end{figure}

The problem appears to be harder than the COIL100 problem, because the persons in the INRIA dataset are more similar to each other than the objects in the other dataset.
